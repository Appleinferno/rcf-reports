Why Engineered Hardwood Is the Best Choice for Multi Level Homes

Multi level residential homes throw a lot at a floor. You have got the basement, the main living area, and the upper floors, each with different humidity levels and subfloor conditions.

Solid hardwood simply cannot handle that kind of variation. It expands and contracts with moisture, which makes it a risky choice below grade or in any area where humidity fluctuates.

Engineered hardwood is built differently. It uses a real slab of hardwood as a wood veneer on top with a layered plywood core underneath. That combination of multiple layers with each wooden ply that are bonded together to create a construction that is more stable no matter where you install it.

This hardwood floor type can be floated for an easy to install in all levels of a house. Engineered flooring can be installed below grade and above grade. That flexibility is exactly what you need when you are flooring an entire multi level house and want a consistent look throughout.

On top of that, you get real wood character. Real grain. Real texture. Nobody walks into a room and says, "That looks like engineered hardwood." They say, "Nice floors."

What to Look For in the Best Engineered Wood Floors for Multi Level Residential Homes

Not all engineered hardwood is the same. Here is what actually matters when you are buying flooring for a multi level home.

  • Wear Layer Thickness: This is the real wood layer on top. Thicker is better, especially in high-traffic areas. We recommend 3mm or more.
  • Plank Width: Wide plank flooring (7 inches and up) is the biggest trend in residential flooring right now. It makes any room look bigger and more open.
  • Finish: Matte and low-gloss finishes are practical. They hide everyday scratches and scuffs far better than high-gloss options.
  • Species: Oak, maple, and hickory are your workhorses. They are dense, durable, and widely available in our discount inventory.
  • First Quality vs. Builder Grade: We carry First Quality flooring. That means no factory seconds or cosmetic rejects mixed in.
  • Installation Method: Nail-down, glue-down, or floating. Engineered hardwood supports all three, which matters when you are dealing with different subfloor types on each level.

Installing Engineered Wood Flooring on Every Level of Your Home

This is where engineered hardwood really separates itself from the competition. Solid hardwood is basically off the table for below-grade installations. Luxury vinyl plank flooring is waterproof, but it does not give you that true hardwood character.

Engineered hardwood sits in the sweet spot. It is easy to install in all levels of a home. It can be installed below grade and above grade, which means you can use the same product from the basement all the way to the second or third floor.

That consistency is a big deal if you want your home to flow visually from level to level.

Here is a quick breakdown of installation methods by level:

  • Below Grade (Basement): Glue-down or floating method. Avoid nail-down over concrete. Make sure moisture levels are within the manufacturer's specs before you start.
  • On Grade (Main Level): All three methods work here. Nail-down over plywood subfloor is the most popular and gives the most solid feel underfoot.
  • Above Grade (Upper Floors): Nail-down or floating both work great. Floating installations can reduce sound transmission between floors if you use a quality underlayment.

We carry engineered hardwood that works with all three installation methods. If you are not sure which method makes sense for your project, give us a call. Someone who actually knows flooring will pick up.

Engineered Hardwood vs. Vinyl Plank for Multi Level Homes

We get this question a lot. Should you go with engineered hardwood or vinyl plank flooring for a multi level home?

Here is the honest answer.

Vinyl plank flooring is 100% waterproof. If you have a bathroom or laundry room on any floor, vinyl plank is going to be the safer call for those specific spaces.

But for the living areas, bedrooms, hallways, and open spaces throughout your multi level home? Engineered hardwood wins on feel, acoustics, and long-term value. Walking on real wood is a different experience than walking on vinyl. You feel it.

Engineered hardwood also adds more resale value. Buyers recognize real wood. They will pay for it.

A lot of our customers do a combination. Engineered hardwood throughout the main living spaces on every level, and vinyl plank in the wet areas. We sell both. We can help you figure out what makes sense for your specific floor plan.
